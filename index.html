Beginner's Guide to Poker

The Absolute Basics

  • Objective: Win chips by having the best hand at showdown OR making everyone else fold
  • Blinds: Forced bets that rotate clockwise each hand to create action
  • Hole Cards: Your two private cards that only you can see
  • Community Cards: Five shared cards dealt face-up (Flop, Turn, River)
  • Best Hand: Use any combination of your 2 cards + 5 community cards to make the best 5-card hand
  • Betting Rounds: Pre-flop, Flop, Turn, River - you can bet, check, call, raise, or fold
  • Showdown: If 2+ players remain after the river, cards are revealed and best hand wins

Your First Session Checklist

  • Buy-in: Bring 100 big blinds minimum (e.g., $200 for $1/$2 game)
  • Seat: Ask the floor for the game you want, they'll call you when ready
  • Chips: Buy chips at the cage or from the dealer when seated
  • Cards: Look at them discreetly, keep them on the table
  • Action: Watch when it's your turn - the dealer will prompt you
  • Announce: Say your action clearly: "call", "raise to $X", "fold"
  • Rake: The house takes a small % of each pot - this is automatic

Starting Hand Selection

  • Premium (Always Play): AA, KK, QQ, JJ, AKs, AKo
  • Strong (Usually Raise): TT, 99, AQs, AQo, AJs, KQs
  • Playable (Position Dependent): 88-22, ATs-A2s, KJs, QJs, JTs, suited connectors
  • Fold Most Hands: If it's not on the above lists, consider folding
  • Tight is Right: As a beginner, play only 15-20% of hands
  • Position Matters: Play tighter in early position, looser on the button
  • Avoid Trap Hands: KJ, QJ, KT, QT offsuit look pretty but lose money

Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Playing too many hands: Boredom leads to bad calls - stay disciplined
  • Calling too much: "I just want to see" costs chips - raise or fold
  • Ignoring position: A hand that's a raise on the button is a fold UTG
  • Overvaluing top pair: Top pair is not the nuts - be careful on scary boards
  • Chasing draws: Don't call big bets hoping to hit - know your odds
  • Playing scared: If losing the buy-in bothers you, play lower stakes
  • Showing cards: Don't show your hand when you fold - give away no info

Basic Betting Strategy

  • Pre-flop raise: 3x the big blind is standard (e.g., $6 in $1/$2)
  • Add for limpers: +1 BB for each player who limped before you
  • Continuation bet: Bet 50-66% of pot on flop if you raised pre-flop
  • Value betting: When you have a good hand, BET - don't slowplay
  • Pot control: With medium hands, keep the pot small
  • Don't min-bet: Tiny bets accomplish nothing - bet with purpose
  • Respect raises: If someone raises big, they usually have it (especially live)

Survival Tips

  • Set a budget: Only bring money you can afford to lose completely
  • Time limit: Set a session time limit - tired players make bad decisions
  • Take breaks: Get up, walk around, clear your head every hour
  • Don't chase losses: If you lose your buy-in, consider leaving
  • Stay sober: Alcohol destroys decision-making at the table
  • Be friendly: A good table image helps; being rude makes you a target
  • It's a marathon: One session doesn't define you - think long-term

Pre-Flop Strategy

  • Position is everything - Play tighter from early position, looser from late position
  • Standard open raise: 2.5-3x BB + 1 BB per limper
  • 3-Bet sizing: 3x in position, 4x out of position
  • Avoid limping - either raise or fold (with rare exceptions)
  • Tighten up against early position raises - they represent strong ranges
  • Premium hands (AA, KK, QQ, AK): Always raise/3-bet
  • Small pocket pairs: Call for set value if getting 15:1 implied odds
  • Suited connectors: Best played in position with deep stacks

Flop Play

  • C-Bet 50-75% pot as the pre-flop raiser on favorable boards
  • Good c-bet boards: Dry (K72 rainbow), Ace-high, Paired boards
  • Check more on: Wet boards (JT9 two-tone), Low connected boards
  • With strong hands: Build the pot early - don't slowplay
  • Draws: Semi-bluff with equity, check-call without position
  • When check-raised: Fold marginal hands, continue with strong hands/draws
  • Board texture matters more than your hand strength alone
  • Count your outs: Flush draw = 9 outs, OESD = 8 outs

Turn Play

  • The turn is where pots get big - be selective
  • Continue betting value hands: 60-75% pot
  • Give up on bluffs that didn't improve (unless you have equity)
  • Pot odds tighten: You need stronger draws to continue
  • Scare cards: Evaluate if the turn card hits opponent's range
  • Check back medium-strength hands for pot control
  • If opponent leads into you: They're often polarized (strong or bluffing)
  • Double-barrel bluffs: Best on cards that favor your range

River Play

  • Value bet thin - many players call too much
  • Bluffs should tell a consistent story from earlier streets
  • Sizing: 60-80% pot for value, vary bluffs
  • Missed draws: Consider if you can represent made hands
  • Don't bluff calling stations - they will call
  • Check back medium hands that can't get called by worse
  • Big river raises are usually very strong (especially live)
  • Calculate pot odds before hero calling

Bet Sizing Guide

  • Small bets (25-40%): Thin value, dry boards, many made hands
  • Medium bets (50-75%): Standard c-bets, balanced ranges
  • Large bets (80-100%+): Polarized (very strong or bluffs)
  • Overbet (>pot): Nutted hands or strong bluffs on scary runouts
  • Size up with value hands when draws complete
  • Size down on dry boards where opponent has few strong hands
  • In live games: Round to easy chip amounts
  • Pay attention to opponent sizing tells

Pot Odds & Math

  • Pot Odds = Call Amount / (Pot + Call Amount)
  • Need pot odds better than your equity to call profitably
  • Flush draw: ~35% equity (9 outs x 4 on flop, x 2 on turn)
  • Open-ended straight: ~32% equity (8 outs)
  • Gutshot: ~16% equity (4 outs)
  • Two overcards: ~24% equity (6 outs)
  • Rule of 4 and 2: Outs x 4 on flop, Outs x 2 on turn
  • Consider implied odds with speculative hands

Reading Opponents

  • Bet sizing tells: Weak = small, Strong = big (for most recreational players)
  • Timing tells: Quick calls often = draws, Long tanks = marginal decisions
  • Watch for physical tells: Chip handling, posture, eye contact
  • Recreational players: Overvalue top pair, hate folding
  • Tight players: Respect their raises, they have it
  • Aggressive players: Give them rope, trap with big hands
  • Note: Online reads don't always transfer to live
  • Build player profiles throughout the session

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Playing too many hands - Discipline is key
  • Calling too much instead of raising/folding
  • Ignoring position when deciding to play a hand
  • Slowplaying big hands (just bet for value!)
  • Chasing draws without proper odds
  • Tilting after bad beats - take breaks when needed
  • Playing scared money - only play stakes you're comfortable with
  • Overvaluing suited hands (being suited adds ~3% equity)

Poker Glossary

Betting Terms

  • Open/Open Raise: The first raise in a betting round (not counting blinds)
  • 2-Bet: The initial raise (open raise). Blinds count as the "1-bet"
  • 3-Bet: A re-raise over the initial raise. NOT 3x the pot - it's the 3rd bet in sequence
  • 4-Bet: A re-raise over a 3-bet. Usually indicates a very strong hand
  • 5-Bet: A re-raise over a 4-bet. Almost always AA or KK (or a bluff)
  • C-Bet (Continuation Bet): Betting the flop after you raised pre-flop
  • Donk Bet: Betting into the pre-flop raiser out of position
  • Probe Bet: Betting when checked to after opponent skipped a c-bet
  • Block Bet: A small bet to prevent a larger bet from opponent
  • Overbet: A bet larger than the pot size

Position & Table Terms

  • UTG (Under the Gun): First to act pre-flop, left of big blind
  • MP (Middle Position): Seats between early and late position
  • HJ (Hijack): Two seats before the button
  • CO (Cutoff): One seat before the button
  • BTN (Button): Best position - acts last post-flop, has dealer button
  • SB (Small Blind): Forced half-bet, first to act post-flop
  • BB (Big Blind): Forced full bet, defends against steals
  • IP (In Position): Acting after your opponent(s)
  • OOP (Out of Position): Acting before your opponent(s)
  • Heads-Up: Only two players remaining in the hand

Hand & Card Terms

  • Suited (s): Two cards of the same suit (e.g., AKs = Ace-King suited)
  • Offsuit (o): Two cards of different suits (e.g., AKo = Ace-King offsuit)
  • Pocket Pair: Two cards of the same rank (e.g., 77, QQ)
  • Broadway: Cards Ten through Ace (T, J, Q, K, A)
  • Connectors: Cards in sequence (e.g., 98, JT)
  • Suited Connectors: Sequential cards of same suit (e.g., 98)
  • Gap/Gapper: Cards with gaps (e.g., J9 = one-gapper)
  • Kicker: Side card that breaks ties (AK vs AQ on A-high board)
  • Nuts: The best possible hand given the board
  • Set: Three of a kind using a pocket pair + one board card
  • Trips: Three of a kind using one hole card + two board cards

Board & Street Terms

  • Flop: First three community cards dealt together
  • Turn: Fourth community card (also called "4th street")
  • River: Fifth and final community card (also called "5th street")
  • Board: All community cards combined
  • Wet Board: Coordinated board with many draws (e.g., JT9)
  • Dry Board: Uncoordinated board with few draws (e.g., K72)
  • Rainbow: Board with all different suits (no flush possible)
  • Monotone: Board with all same suit (e.g., A73)
  • Paired Board: Board with a pair (e.g., K-K-7)
  • Runout: The turn and river cards together

Draw & Equity Terms

  • Draw: A hand that needs improvement to win
  • Outs: Cards remaining in deck that complete your hand
  • Flush Draw: Four cards to a flush (9 outs)
  • OESD (Open-Ended Straight Draw): Four consecutive cards (8 outs)
  • Gutshot: Inside straight draw needing one specific card (4 outs)
  • Backdoor Draw: Needing two cards to complete (e.g., runner-runner flush)
  • Equity: Your mathematical share of the pot based on winning chances
  • Pot Odds: Ratio of pot size to the bet you must call
  • Implied Odds: Potential future winnings if you hit your draw
  • Reverse Implied Odds: Potential future losses when you hit but are beaten

Action & Play Terms

  • Limp: Just calling the big blind pre-flop (generally bad)
  • Isolate: Raising to play heads-up against a limper
  • Squeeze: 3-betting after a raise and one or more callers
  • Float: Calling a bet with the plan to bluff later
  • Slow Roll: Deliberately taking time to show a winning hand (very bad etiquette)
  • Check-Raise: Checking then raising when opponent bets
  • Jam/Shove: Going all-in
  • Fold Equity: The value gained from the chance opponent folds
  • Hero Call: Calling with a marginal hand believing opponent is bluffing
  • Showdown Value: A hand good enough to win at showdown without betting

Player Types & Styles

  • TAG (Tight-Aggressive): Plays few hands but bets/raises them strongly
  • LAG (Loose-Aggressive): Plays many hands aggressively
  • Nit: Extremely tight player who only plays premium hands
  • Fish/Recreational: Inexperienced or losing player
  • Calling Station: Player who calls too much, hard to bluff
  • Maniac: Extremely aggressive, bets and raises constantly
  • Shark: Skilled, winning player
  • Rock: Very tight, passive player
  • Reg (Regular): Professional or semi-pro who plays consistently
  • Whale: Wealthy recreational player who plays high stakes

General Terms

  • Range: All possible hands a player could have in a spot
  • Polarized: A range of very strong hands and bluffs (no medium)
  • Merged/Linear: A range including strong and medium hands
  • GTO (Game Theory Optimal): Mathematically balanced, unexploitable play
  • Exploitative: Deviating from GTO to exploit specific opponent tendencies
  • EV (Expected Value): Long-term average outcome of a decision
  • Variance: Natural ups and downs due to luck
  • Tilt: Playing emotionally after bad beats or frustration
  • Cooler: Losing with a very strong hand to an even stronger hand
  • Bad Beat: Losing when you were a heavy favorite to win

Transitioning from Online to Live Poker

Key Differences

  • Pace: Live games are 3-4x slower (25-30 hands/hr vs 75-100 online)
  • Players: Generally softer competition at equivalent stakes
  • Rake: Often higher but capped - check the room's structure
  • Stack depth: Often deeper stacks in live cash games
  • Multi-tabling: You can only play one table - stay focused
  • No HUD: You must track stats mentally or with notes
  • Social element: Table talk and physical tells matter

Live Poker Etiquette

  • Protect your cards: Use a card protector or hands
  • Act in turn: Wait for your turn before acting
  • Verbal is binding: If you say "raise" you must raise
  • One chip rule: A single chip is usually a call
  • String bets: Put all chips out at once or announce
  • Tip dealers: $1-2 per pot won is customary
  • Keep cards visible: On the table and in front of chips
  • Don't slow roll: Show winning hands promptly

Live Player Tendencies

  • More limping and calling - punish with larger raises
  • Less aggression overall - you can value bet thinner
  • Rarely bluff the river - believe big river raises
  • Overvalue hands like top pair - get value from them
  • More likely to show emotion and give tells
  • Often play too many hands - be patient, wait for spots
  • Recreational players just want to play - they will call

Adjustments to Make

  • Open larger: 4-5x BB is standard in many live games
  • Isolate limpers: Raise to 3x + 1x per limper
  • Value bet more: Players call wider than online
  • Bluff less: Especially on the river vs calling stations
  • Pay attention: Use slow pace to study opponents
  • Manage bankroll: Variance hits harder when playing fewer hands
  • Stay patient: Don't force action due to boredom

Physical Tells to Watch

  • Shaking hands: Often indicates a big hand (adrenaline)
  • Quick glance at chips: Usually means they want to bet
  • Leaning forward: Often shows interest/strength
  • Covering mouth: Can indicate nervousness (bluffing)
  • Staring at you: Often a sign of weakness trying to intimidate
  • Looking away: Can indicate strength (feigning disinterest)
  • Note: Tells are player-specific - establish baselines first

Bankroll & Session Tips

  • Recommended bankroll: 20-30 buy-ins for cash games
  • Buy in full: Start with 100BB+ to maximize edge
  • Set stop losses: 2-3 buy-ins per session max loss
  • Take breaks: Get up every 1-2 hours to stay fresh
  • Stay hydrated: Avoid too much alcohol at the table
  • Quit when tired: Fatigue destroys decision-making
  • Table select: Move if your table is too tough
